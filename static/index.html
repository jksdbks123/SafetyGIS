Help & Data Reference

About GIS-Track

GIS-Track is a web-based transportation safety GIS tool covering all of California.

It combines three data sources on a single interactive map:

  • CHP CCRS crash records (2019–2024)
  • OpenStreetMap infrastructure (roads, signals, sidewalks, bike lanes…)
  • Mapillary street-level detected signs

Use the layer controls on the left to show/hide data. Click any feature to inspect it. Draw a fence or click features to build a selection, then export as GeoJSON.

Developed by Zhihui as a personal vibe coding exercise. Not an official product.

Crash Data (CCRS)

Source: California Highway Patrol (CHP) Crash Cause Reporting System, via data.ca.gov. Updated daily.

Coverage: All 58 California counties • Years: 2019–2024

Loading: Data is cached per county on first viewport visit. A blue banner at the bottom means a background fetch is in progress — the map will refresh automatically when done.

Severity classification:

BadgeMeaning
FatalOne or more persons killed
Severe InjuryVictim Degree of Injury = 2 (severe)
InjuryDegree 3 (visible) or 4 (complaint of pain)
PDOProperty damage only — no injury recorded

Filters: Use the "Pedestrian involved" and "Cyclist involved" checkboxes under Crash Points to narrow the visible crashes. These are derived from the MotorVehicleInvolvedWithCode field (B=Pedestrian, E=Bicycle) and are available immediately from cached crash data.

Key fields:

FieldDescription
collision_typeType of crash (head-on, sideswipe, rear-end, etc.)
primary_collision_factorPrimary cause determined by officer
weather_1Weather at time of crash
road_condition_1Road surface condition
lighting_descriptionLighting conditions
primary_road / secondary_roadStreet names at crash location
is_freewayWhether crash occurred on a freeway
killed / injuredTotal killed and injured in the crash
Parties Data

Source: CCRS Parties table — one record per vehicle or person involved in each crash.

A crash with 2 cars has 2 Party records. A crash involving a pedestrian has a Party record with Party Type = Pedestrian.

Visible in: click any crash point → Parties tab in the popup.

Key fields:

FieldDescription
Party TypeDriver, Pedestrian, Bicyclist, Parked Vehicle, Other
At FaultY/N — fault assignment by investigating officer
Party Age / SexDemographics of the driver or pedestrian
Party SobrietyImpairment status (had not been drinking / had been drinking / impaired / etc.)
Vehicle Make / YearVehicle involved
Movement Preceding CrashWhat the party was doing before impact
InattentionSpecific distraction type if applicable

The has_pedestrian, has_cyclist, and has_impaired summary flags in the crash popup are derived from Parties data and power the crash filter checkboxes.

Victims Data (InjuredWitnessPassengers)

Source: CCRS InjuredWitnessPassengers table — one record per injured person, witness, or passenger.

A vehicle with 1 driver and 2 passengers that are all injured generates 3 Victim records. This is distinct from the Parties table (which represents the vehicle as one record).

Visible in: click any crash point → Victims tab in the popup.

Degree of Injury scale:

CodeMeaning
1Killed
2Severe Injury
3Other Visible Injury
4Complaint of Pain
5Possible Injury
6No Apparent Injury
0Not a Victim (witness only)

Key fields: Victim Role, Victim Ejected, Victim Safety Equipment 1 & 2 (seat belt, helmet, airbag use), Victim Seating Position, Victim Age/Sex.

The crash severity badge uses the minimum Degree of Injury across all victims — more accurate than the text-based fallback used for older records.

How to Use

Layer toggles — click any row in the left panel to show/hide that layer.

Crash filters — enable the Crash Points layer, then use the checkboxes to narrow to pedestrian-involved, cyclist-involved, or impaired-driver crashes. Multiple checkboxes are AND-combined.

Inspect a feature — click any point or line on the map. The popup shows all data. For crash points, use the Crash / Parties / Victims tabs to navigate the three CCRS tables.

Click to select — clicking a feature also adds it to the selection. Cmd/Ctrl+click appends without replacing the current selection.

Draw fence — use Rect or Polygon in the Select & Export panel to draw a selection area. Only features on visible layers are captured.

Export

  • Crashes Only — exports all selected features with their raw CCRS / OSM properties as a GeoJSON file.
  • With Parties & Victims — same as above, but each crash feature also includes nested parties and victims arrays from the two related CCRS tables.

Basemap — toggle between Map (OpenFreeMap) and Satellite (Esri World Imagery) at the top of the panel.

Street View — drag the yellow person icon onto the map, or click it to activate placement mode, then click your desired location.

Stats ribbon — click the ▲ tab at the bottom of the screen to expand feature counts for the current viewport.
